10-year cardiovascular disease risk

Framingham CVD Risk Score
Estimates 10-year risk of cardiovascular events including coronary death, MI, stroke, and heart failure. Based on the D'Agostino et al. 2008 general CVD risk model — validated for Canadian populations and referenced by CCS dyslipidemia guidelines. Cholesterol values in mmol/L.
—

Risk categories per CCS guidelines: Low <10%  ·  Moderate 10–19%  ·  High ≥20%. Consider doubling FRS if positive family history of premature CAD (1st-degree male <55, female <65).

Lipoprotein(a) cardiovascular risk interpreter

Lp(a) Risk Assessment
Lp(a) is an independent, primarily genetic cardiovascular risk factor not captured by the Framingham score. Elevated levels affect approximately 20% of the population and increase risk of MI, stroke, and aortic stenosis — particularly relevant post-menopause, when Lp(a) may rise. A single lifetime measurement is sufficient as levels are ~90% genetically determined and stable.
Normal: <75 nmol/L  /  <30 mg/dL Borderline: 75–124 nmol/L High: 125–199 nmol/L  /  ≥50 mg/dL Very high: ≥200 nmol/L

Clinical considerations

Risk estimates based on Mendelian randomization data (Björnson et al., UK Biobank, n=502,413): OR 1.28 per 50 nmol/L Lp(a). EAS / ACC / AHA thresholds applied. This tool is a clinical reference aid, not a diagnostic instrument.

FRAX and Tyrer-Cuzick use proprietary, country-specific models that cannot be reliably reproduced independently. They are linked rather than built in to ensure you are always using the validated, up-to-date versions maintained by their respective organisations.

Why do different Framingham tools give different numbers?

Different sites produce slightly different scores because they use different algorithm versions — the 2008 lipid model, the BMI-based model, or older point-scoring tables — and different unit conversions. The built-in calculator here uses the 2008 D'Agostino general CVD risk model, the version validated for Canadian populations and referenced by CCS dyslipidemia guidelines.
